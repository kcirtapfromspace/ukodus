Solving Techniques

The Ukodus engine uses 22 human-style solving techniques to rate and solve every puzzle. Each technique has a Sudoku Explainer (SE) difficulty rating. Here's the full catalog, organized by family.

Singles

The foundation of all Sudoku solving. A single is a cell or candidate that can be determined directly, with no complex reasoning required.

Technique SE Rating Tier
Hidden Single 1.5 Beginner
Naked Single 2.3 Easy

Pairs & Triples

Subset techniques that identify groups of candidates locked within a set of cells. Naked subsets restrict other cells; hidden subsets restrict the cells themselves.

Technique SE Rating Tier
Naked Pair 3.0 Intermediate
Hidden Pair 3.4 Intermediate
Naked Triple 3.6 Intermediate
Hidden Triple 3.8 Hard
Naked Quad 5.0 Expert
Hidden Quad 5.4 Master

Intersections

When candidates for a digit in a box are confined to a single row or column (or vice versa), the intersection eliminates candidates elsewhere in that line or box.

Technique SE Rating Tier
Pointing Pair 2.6 Medium
Box/Line Reduction 2.8 Hard

Fish

Fish patterns generalize the X-Wing concept: N rows (or columns) contain a digit in exactly N columns (or rows), allowing eliminations. Variants include finned, franken, siamese, and mutant configurations.

Technique SE Rating Tier
X-Wing 3.2 Intermediate
Finned X-Wing 3.4 Expert
Swordfish 3.8 Expert
Finned Swordfish 4.0 Expert
Jellyfish 5.2 Expert
Finned Jellyfish 5.4 Expert
Siamese Fish 5.5 Master
Franken Fish 5.5 Master
Mutant Fish 6.5 Extreme
Kraken Fish 8.0 Extreme

Wings

Wing patterns exploit bivalue and trivalue cells linked by shared candidates. A pivot cell connects two wing cells, creating eliminations where the wings overlap.

Technique SE Rating Tier
XY-Wing 4.2 Expert
XYZ-Wing 4.4 Expert
W-Wing 4.4 Master
WXYZ-Wing 4.6 Expert

Chains

Chain techniques build alternating inference chains through strong and weak links. They connect candidates across the grid, proving eliminations through logical contradiction.

Technique SE Rating Tier
X-Chain 4.5 Master
3D Medusa 5.0 Master
AIC 6.0 Master

Rectangles

Uniqueness-based techniques that exploit the constraint that a valid Sudoku must have a single solution. A "deadly rectangle" pattern would create two solutions, so at least one candidate can be eliminated.

Technique SE Rating Tier
Empty Rectangle 4.6 Expert
Avoidable Rectangle 4.6 Expert
Unique Rectangle 4.6 Expert
Hidden Rectangle 4.7 Expert
Extended Unique Rectangle 5.5 Master

ALS

Almost Locked Sets (ALS) are groups of N cells containing N+1 candidates. By linking two or more ALS through restricted common candidates, powerful eliminations emerge.

Technique SE Rating Tier
ALS-XZ 5.5 Master
ALS-XY-Wing 7.0 Extreme
ALS Chain 7.5 Extreme

Forcing

Forcing chain techniques test all candidates of a cell or region. If every possibility leads to the same conclusion, that conclusion must hold. The most powerful logical techniques before brute force.

Technique SE Rating Tier
Nishio Forcing Chain 7.5 Extreme
Cell Forcing Chain 8.3 Extreme
Region Forcing Chain 8.5 Extreme
Dynamic Forcing Chain 9.3 Extreme

Other

Specialized techniques that don't fit neatly into the families above. Includes set-based eliminations, aligned exclusions, uniqueness shortcuts, and the brute-force fallback.

Technique SE Rating Tier
Sue de Coq 5.0 Master
Aligned Pair Exclusion 6.2 Extreme
Aligned Triplet Exclusion 7.5 Extreme
Death Blossom 8.5 Extreme
BUG+1 5.6 Master
Backtracking 11.0 Extreme
